THE ORIGIN OF 67

How "67 Online" Became a Legend

The "67 online" meme originated from a livestream by Destiny (Steven Kenneth Bonnell II), a popular political streamer and commentator.

During one of his streams, Destiny noticed a viewer with the username "67" who appeared to be constantly watching his stream, regardless of the time of day or night.

Destiny began commenting on how "67" was always online, leading to chat joining in on the joke. The meme evolved into a community in-joke about this perpetually online viewer.

The phrase "67 online" became shorthand for someone who spends an excessive amount of time on the internet or in streaming communities.

Over time, "67" transformed from a specific username into a symbolic representation of the always-online viewer in streaming culture.
